Social Intuition Privacy Policy

Policy Version: 1.0

Publish Date: 2015-12-30

 

Social Intuition has a primary goal to provide you, the user, with fun way to capture, share and track your life intuitions. Your intuitions may pertain to anything such as school, home, work, personal; they may be light-hearted, social or serious. Privacy of your intuitions may be set with a visibility level you set. Setting an intuition to have private visibility allows only you to view it; cohort visibility allows you and your cohorts to view and comment/like your intuition; public visibility allows anyone on the Internet to see your intuition. Of utmost importance � your privacy is respected and of top concern and therefore, Social Intuition provides various visibility settings for your discretion of privacy. Please review below to learn specifics.

 

Intuition Privacy

Each user with an account may have multiple intuitions. Intuitions may have one of three various �visibility� levels that control privacy. The differences are as follows:

1.     Private visibility: Allows only you to view the intuition.

2.     Cohort visibility: Allows you and approved cohorts (friends) to view the intuition.

3.     Public visibility: Allows everyone on the Internet to view the intuition.

 

Information Collected

The following information is collected during registration through Social Intuition or third party user information imports:

1.     First and last name: Used to assign a standard human-readable name to an individual.

2.     Username: Used to assign a unique identifier in the profile URL belonging to a Social Intuition user.

3.     Email: For us to communicate in case of a forgotten password, legal updates or occasional Social Intuition reminders and promotions.

4.     Other information: If provided in third party, then basic information (e.g. gender, time zone) is collected to better provide meaningful content for a user.

 

Cookies

Social Intuition uses cookies minimally. Currently cookies are used to reestablish the session. They do not store or pass user data. And these cookies are only passed over an SSL (encrypted) connection. There are two cookies that you will find in your browser�s internals. They are as follows:

1.     ssk: This is a �remember me� cookie that allows a user to establish a secure, logged-in session with the server after previously closing the browser and revisiting, without needing to re-login. The cookie is established after a user has logged-in for either the first time or after having last deleted cookies. The intent is to provide secure method to quickly revisit a protected page. Without this, or if you block cookies, then the user will need to sign-in after re-opening the browser before visiting a secured page. If blocked, this will be an inconvenience.

2.     JSESSIONID: This cookie is used to track active user data while active on the server, primarily for caching purposes in serving pages efficiently. It expires when the browser closes. If this cookie is blocked or removed, it will redirect the user to the login page with every click to a protected page. This is more a necessity but the site will permit limited usage if the cookie is removed although an inconvenience.
